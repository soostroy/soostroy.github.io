About Us

Discover the rich flavors of Middle Eastern cuisine on our website dedicated to all things culinary from the region. From classic dishes like hummus, falafel, and shawarma to lesser-known specialties, we've got you covered. Our website features a wide range of recipes that will delight your taste buds and transport you to the streets of Beirut, Jerusalem, or Cairo. Our expert food writers and chefs are passionate about Middle Eastern cuisine and are constantly working to bring you the latest news, recipes, and insights. Whether you're an experienced cook or a beginner, our easy-to-follow recipes and tutorials will help you recreate your favorite Middle Eastern dishes in your own kitchen.

Our website is not just about food; it's also a treasure trove of information about Middle Eastern culture and history. We offer features and articles on traditional music, art, festivals, and celebrations from the region. By exploring the rich and diverse world of Middle Eastern cuisine, you'll gain a deeper understanding of the people, traditions, and customs that make up this fascinating region. Our goal is to help you appreciate and enjoy Middle Eastern cuisine to the fullest, whether you're cooking at home or dining out at a local Middle Eastern restaurant. So come and join us on a culinary journey through the flavors of the Middle East!

Kebbah Be-Laban

Middle Eastern dish made of ground beef or lamb, bulgur wheat, and spices. The mixture is shaped into balls and can be fried or baked. It's known for its savory flavor and is often served with yogurt or a tahini-based sauce.

Mansaf

traditional Jordanian dish made with lamb, rice, and tangy jameed sauce. It's a symbol of hospitality and generosity, often served at special occasions. The lamb is slow-cooked with spices and then layered on top of the rice, with the jameed sauce poured over everything. Mansaf is typically eaten with your hands and is a must-try for anyone visiting Jordan.

$8

Kabsa

The fragrant, spiced rice is often mixed with tender chicken or lamb, creating a delicious and filling meal. The blend of spices like cardamom, cumin, and saffron give Kabsa its unique flavor, and it's often served with sides like salad, pickles, and yogurt.
